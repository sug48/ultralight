Almost Linguine and Clams

On Tuesday, January 16th, 2024, I wanted soup for dinner because it was cold and snowy, but didn't get started early enough soaking beans and making veggie stock.

Craving comfort but needing something that would cook quickly, I settled on a soupy linguine with clams, even though I didn't have linguine on hand. Or clams, or wine, or herbs.

Follow these steps carefully and closely to make a dish that is almost linguine and clams, precisely like I did.

Ingredients

  • A dozen clams per person
  • Smoked blue fish, 1/3 pound
  • Pasta, 1 opened box
  • Garlic, whatever's left
  • Butter, unmelted
  • Olive oil
  • Dry white wine
  • Gremolata
    (Usually you're supposed to use fresh herbs like parsely. I think basil is nice too in that case, even some capers.)

Preparation

  1. On the way back from the gym, stop at the wine store and get some wine. Ask for a bottle of Italian stuff you won't feel bad cooking with. It's the guy at the register's first day, so make encouraing small talk.
  2. Stop at the fish market. Get clams, a piece of smoked bluefish, and frozen gremolata.
  3. Shovel the walkway.
  4. Take a shower.
  5. Put on some jazz! Don't skip this step.
  6. Make 2 to 4 oz of pasta per person. What do you have around? Orchiette? How about orchiette? Italian for little ears. It's supposed to be linguine, but a bite size pasta is better for a soupy dish anyway. Say something kind to the ears.
  7. Heat a reasonable amount of olive oil and butter in a 12-inch pan with a matching lid. Set the lid aside.
  8. Add chopped garlic, I don't know like a couple cloves?
  9. Bring 1/2 cup of wine for each dozen clams to a simmer.
  10. Eat some of the smoked blue fish directly off the paper it's wrapped in.
  11. Rinse the clams. Use your fingers to scrape away grit.
  12. Add some salt and a spoonful of gremolata to the pan. Eyeball it, how's it looking? Maybe a little more wine? Eat some blue fish.
  13. Add clams to the pan being careful not to splash your liquid everywhere. Cover until you hear popping subside. Those are the clams.
  14. Take clams out of pan. Dump pasta in the sauce and let it hang out in there for a little bit, warming up. Remember: soupy. Add a little water or wine if you need to. Remove from heat and throw in a big pat of butter.
  15. Pour the soupy pasta in a big bowl. Put all the clams on top.
